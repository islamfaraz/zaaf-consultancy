// 04 — process

How every engagement runs

The same five phases, every time. The scope changes; the discipline does not.

  1. 01

    Discovery

    We spend the first week listening, not presenting. Structured interviews across engineering, operations, and leadership to map the actual problem — not the assumed one.

    Anti-pattern avoided: skipping discovery to "save time" and scoping the wrong problem at pace.

  2. 02

    Architecture

    A written architecture document, shared before any code is written. Decisions documented, alternatives considered, trade-offs explicit. You review it; we revise it; then we build it.

    Anti-pattern avoided: building first and documenting never, leaving tribal knowledge as the only map.

  3. 03

    Delivery

    Two-week sprints with visible progress, written updates, and no surprises. Your engineers are involved throughout — pair programming, review sessions, and knowledge transfer are part of every sprint, not an add-on at the end.

    Anti-pattern avoided: disappearing for six weeks and returning with something that misses the brief.

  4. 04

    Handover

    A formal handover sprint. Full documentation, runbooks, architecture diagrams, and a structured knowledge transfer programme. We measure success by whether your team can operate the system without us.

    Anti-pattern avoided: handover as a single 90-minute call with a shared drive link and a goodbye email.

  5. 05

    Retained (Optional)

    For organisations that want ongoing engineering support, we offer a retained arrangement: a named engineer, defined capacity per month, monthly service review, and a direct line — not a ticket portal.

    Anti-pattern avoided: open-ended retained contracts with no defined scope and no exit criteria.
